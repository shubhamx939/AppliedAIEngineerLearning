## What this lesson is really about

Managed AI platforms promise acceleration, but they each make tradeoffs around flexibility, observability, pricing, and integration depth. This lesson helps you read that landscape clearly.

Platform choices become expensive to reverse, so you want a grounded view before the product depends on provider-specific abstractions.

## What you will understand

- Compare cloud AI platforms without reducing the choice to branding.
- Understand the tradeoff between managed convenience and architectural lock-in.
- Reason about governance, cost, and deployment ergonomics together.
- Map local prototypes to cloud production paths more realistically.

## Why this topic matters in real AI engineering

Survey the main cloud AI platforms and how they package compute, model access, orchestration, and enterprise controls. This lesson should help you notice which parts are conceptual, which parts are operational, and which parts are the usual source of failure when a team tries to move too quickly.

## How to use this lesson

Read the sections in order the first time. On later passes, use the subtopic map below as a reference spine: it is arranged to help you connect ideas rather than memorize isolated terms.
