## What this lesson is really about

RAG is not a single trick. It is a set of retrieval, storage, ranking, and generation decisions that either cooperate well or quietly sabotage each other.

Most failures blamed on generation are often retrieval or chunking problems upstream. This lesson helps you see the whole path.

## What you will understand

- Explain the full RAG pipeline from ingestion to answer assembly.
- Choose chunking and ranking strategies with clearer tradeoff awareness.
- Diagnose retrieval failures instead of blaming the model generically.
- Design grounded knowledge experiences that stay maintainable.

## Why this topic matters in real AI engineering

Understand retrieval-augmented generation as a full knowledge system: indexing, chunking, ranking, grounding, and answer synthesis. This lesson should help you notice which parts are conceptual, which parts are operational, and which parts are the usual source of failure when a team tries to move too quickly.

## How to use this lesson

Read the sections in order the first time. On later passes, use the subtopic map below as a reference spine: it is arranged to help you connect ideas rather than memorize isolated terms.
