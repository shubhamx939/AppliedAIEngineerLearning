## What this lesson is really about

Fine-tuning is expensive in the wrong hands and extremely powerful in the right context. The trick is to treat it as a data-and-objective problem first, not a hardware flex.

Many teams tune too early, or tune without clear evaluation criteria. This lesson aims to make fine-tuning a deliberate engineering choice.

## What you will understand

- Distinguish when prompting is enough and when tuning is justified.
- Understand LoRA, adapters, and PEFT as memory and optimization choices.
- Prepare training data with stronger task alignment.
- Reason about quality, cost, and deployment consequences of tuning.

## Why this topic matters in real AI engineering

Learn when and how to fine-tune modern models with parameter-efficient methods, data discipline, and clear expectations about gains. This lesson should help you notice which parts are conceptual, which parts are operational, and which parts are the usual source of failure when a team tries to move too quickly.

## How to use this lesson

Read the sections in order the first time. On later passes, use the subtopic map below as a reference spine: it is arranged to help you connect ideas rather than memorize isolated terms.
