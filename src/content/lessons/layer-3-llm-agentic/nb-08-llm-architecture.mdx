## What this lesson is really about

This lesson is about turning LLMs from magical APIs into systems you can inspect. Once you understand what consumes memory, what limits context, and how inference is optimized, model behavior becomes less mysterious.

The product choices around prompts, retrieval, serving, and fine-tuning all depend on what is happening inside the model at inference time.

## What you will understand

- Explain how modern transformer models spend compute and memory.
- Understand the major inference tradeoffs behind latency and quality.
- Read context window and tokenizer behavior more critically.
- Compare model families and alignment strategies with better judgment.

## Why this topic matters in real AI engineering

Open up the black box of modern LLMs: transformer internals, context windows, KV cache, quantization, inference optimization, and alignment. This lesson should help you notice which parts are conceptual, which parts are operational, and which parts are the usual source of failure when a team tries to move too quickly.

## How to use this lesson

Read the sections in order the first time. On later passes, use the subtopic map below as a reference spine: it is arranged to help you connect ideas rather than memorize isolated terms.
