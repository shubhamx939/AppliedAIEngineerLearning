## What this lesson is really about

LLM products are hard to improve when every judgment stays qualitative. Evaluation and observability give you a language for quality, failure, and change over time.

This lesson matters because prompt tweaks, retrieval changes, and model swaps all look promising until a disciplined measurement system says otherwise.

## What you will understand

- Design evaluation sets that reflect real product behavior.
- Track operational metrics without losing task-level quality signals.
- Recognize the difference between anecdotal wins and real regressions.
- Understand observability as a feedback loop, not a logging checklist.

## Why this topic matters in real AI engineering

Build a rigorous evaluation and observability mindset for LLM systems across quality, cost, latency, and regressions. This lesson should help you notice which parts are conceptual, which parts are operational, and which parts are the usual source of failure when a team tries to move too quickly.

## How to use this lesson

Read the sections in order the first time. On later passes, use the subtopic map below as a reference spine: it is arranged to help you connect ideas rather than memorize isolated terms.
