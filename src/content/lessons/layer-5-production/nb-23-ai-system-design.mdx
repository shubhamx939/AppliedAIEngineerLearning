## What this lesson is really about

AI system design is the place where all prior lessons meet. You are no longer learning isolated techniques; you are composing product behavior under real constraints.

The skill here is not drawing big diagrams. It is defending why a system should be shaped one way instead of another.

## What you will understand

- Break AI products into understandable services and responsibilities.
- Communicate tradeoffs around latency, quality, cost, and reliability.
- Recognize how retrieval, serving, state, and evaluation interact.
- Make architecture choices that remain explainable under growth.

## Why this topic matters in real AI engineering

Practice AI system design as product architecture: decomposition, constraints, failure surfaces, and tradeoff communication. This lesson should help you notice which parts are conceptual, which parts are operational, and which parts are the usual source of failure when a team tries to move too quickly.

## How to use this lesson

Read the sections in order the first time. On later passes, use the subtopic map below as a reference spine: it is arranged to help you connect ideas rather than memorize isolated terms.
