## What this lesson is really about

Safety work becomes practical when you stop treating it as a vague alignment topic and start treating it as layered risk management for a product with real users.

Strong guardrails balance usefulness and protection. The engineering challenge is to be strict where risk demands it and graceful where the user still needs flow.

## What you will understand

- Recognize different risk classes in modern LLM applications.
- Design layered defenses rather than relying on one mechanism.
- Think about prompt injection, misuse, and harmful output with more structure.
- Connect safety choices to UX and operational workflows.

## Why this topic matters in real AI engineering

Understand guardrails and AI safety as layered product engineering across policy, prompting, model behavior, and human process. This lesson should help you notice which parts are conceptual, which parts are operational, and which parts are the usual source of failure when a team tries to move too quickly.

## How to use this lesson

Read the sections in order the first time. On later passes, use the subtopic map below as a reference spine: it is arranged to help you connect ideas rather than memorize isolated terms.
