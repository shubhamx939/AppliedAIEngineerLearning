## What this lesson is really about

Multimodal AI expands the interface of the system, but it also multiplies the design and operations questions. Inputs, context, latency, and evaluation all become more complicated.

This lesson matters because the next generation of useful AI products increasingly mixes text, image, audio, and structured context instead of relying on one channel alone.

## What you will understand

- Explain how multimodal systems combine or align different input spaces.
- Understand the product implications of moving beyond text-only models.
- Recognize the extra evaluation and serving complexity multimodal systems add.
- Compare model capabilities through actual task demands, not demos.

## Why this topic matters in real AI engineering

Understand multimodal AI as a systems problem spanning representation, alignment, inference cost, and product interaction design. This lesson should help you notice which parts are conceptual, which parts are operational, and which parts are the usual source of failure when a team tries to move too quickly.

## How to use this lesson

Read the sections in order the first time. On later passes, use the subtopic map below as a reference spine: it is arranged to help you connect ideas rather than memorize isolated terms.
