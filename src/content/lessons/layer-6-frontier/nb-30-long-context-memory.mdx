## What this lesson is really about

As context windows expand, the design question shifts from can I fit more tokens to should I solve this with retrieval, memory, graphs, or brute-force context at all.

This lesson matters because many product teams confuse bigger windows with solved memory. In practice, persistence and relevance are still architecture choices.

## What you will understand

- Compare retrieval, compression, memory, and long-context expansion strategies.
- Understand what long context solves and what it still does not solve.
- Reason about graph-enhanced retrieval and relational memory systems.
- Choose the right persistence strategy for different AI product behaviors.

## Why this topic matters in real AI engineering

Study long-context systems, memory architectures, and GraphRAG as competing and complementary answers to knowledge persistence. This lesson should help you notice which parts are conceptual, which parts are operational, and which parts are the usual source of failure when a team tries to move too quickly.

## How to use this lesson

Read the sections in order the first time. On later passes, use the subtopic map below as a reference spine: it is arranged to help you connect ideas rather than memorize isolated terms.
