## What this lesson is really about

Deep learning starts to make sense when you stop seeing it as stacks of layers and start seeing it as a sequence of tensor transformations, gradient signals, and memory tradeoffs.

This is the lesson where model training becomes something you can reason about, debug, and optimize instead of just running from a recipe.

## What you will understand

- Explain forward pass, backpropagation, and gradient flow in plain language.
- Build reliable PyTorch training loops with real safeguards.
- Understand attention and transformer components beyond diagram-level intuition.
- Debug common training failures such as NaNs, OOMs, and dead gradients.

## Why this topic matters in real AI engineering

Move from high-level neural network familiarity to engineering fluency with PyTorch, attention, transformers, and GPU-aware training loops. This lesson should help you notice which parts are conceptual, which parts are operational, and which parts are the usual source of failure when a team tries to move too quickly.

## How to use this lesson

Read the sections in order the first time. On later passes, use the subtopic map below as a reference spine: it is arranged to help you connect ideas rather than memorize isolated terms.
